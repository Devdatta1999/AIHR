# Nimbus Labs — Employee Handbook & HR Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Reviewed:** April 2026
**Owner:** People Operations

This handbook is the single source of truth for HR policies at Nimbus Labs. The
HR Queries assistant is grounded against this document; if anything answered by
the assistant conflicts with this handbook, the handbook takes precedence.

---

## 1. Working Hours & Remote Work

### 1.1 Standard hours
The standard work week is 40 hours, Monday through Friday. Core collaboration
hours are 10:00 AM – 4:00 PM in the employee's primary time zone. Outside core
hours, employees may flex their schedule subject to manager approval.

### 1.2 Flexible start times
Employees may start their day any time between **7:00 AM and 10:00 AM** local
time. Lunch breaks are unpaid and typically 30–60 minutes.

### 1.3 Remote work
Nimbus Labs operates a hybrid model. Full-time employees may work remotely
**up to 3 days per week** with manager approval. Two anchor days per week
(typically Tuesday and Thursday) are designated as in-office collaboration days
for most teams; managers may adjust anchor days for their team.

Fully remote arrangements are available for specific roles only and require
written approval from the department head and People Operations.

### 1.4 Overtime
Non-exempt employees are paid 1.5x base hourly rate for hours worked over 40
in a week. All overtime requires manager pre-approval.

---

## 2. Paid Time Off (PTO) & Vacation

### 2.1 Annual vacation
Full-time employees accrue **20 paid vacation days** per calendar year. PTO
accrues monthly at 1.67 days per month. New hires begin accruing on their start
date and may take vacation after completing the 90-day onboarding period.

### 2.2 Carryover
Up to **5 unused vacation days** may be carried into the next calendar year.
Days beyond the cap are forfeited on December 31 unless management grants a
written extension.

### 2.3 Holidays
Nimbus Labs observes 11 paid company holidays each year, listed on the
intranet. Employees working on a holiday by request receive holiday pay plus
1.5x their daily rate.

---

## 3. Sick Leave

### 3.1 Annual sick leave entitlement
Full-time employees receive **12 paid sick days per calendar year**. Sick days
are available from the first day of employment and do not require accrual.

### 3.2 Carryover
**Up to 5 unused sick days** may be carried over into the following year. Days
above this cap are not paid out and are forfeited on December 31.

### 3.3 Notification
Employees must notify their direct manager as early as possible on the day of
absence — by email, Slack, or phone before 9:00 AM where practical. A doctor's
note is required for absences of 3 or more consecutive days.

### 3.4 Extended illness
Absences exceeding the 12-day annual sick leave allowance transition to
short-term disability under the company's group plan (see Section 7.4).

---

## 4. Parental Leave

### 4.1 Eligibility
All full-time employees who have completed 90 days of employment are eligible
for paid parental leave following the birth, adoption, or legal placement of
a child.

### 4.2 Duration & pay
Eligible employees receive **16 weeks of fully paid parental leave**.
An additional **8 weeks of unpaid leave** may be requested with manager and
People Operations approval, extending total time off to up to 24 weeks.

### 4.3 Notice period
Employees must notify People Operations **at least 30 days in advance** of
their planned leave start date, except in cases of medical emergency or
unexpected adoption placement.

### 4.4 Return-to-work
Upon return, employees are guaranteed the same role or a comparable position
at the same level and pay. Phased-return schedules (part-time for the first 4
weeks back) are available on request.

---

## 5. Bereavement, Jury Duty & Other Leave

### 5.1 Bereavement leave
Up to **5 paid days** for the death of an immediate family member (spouse,
domestic partner, child, parent, sibling, grandparent, in-laws). Up to 1 paid
day for extended family.

### 5.2 Jury duty
Paid in full for the duration of jury service. Employees should forward the
jury summons to their manager within 48 hours of receipt.

### 5.3 Military leave
Handled per USERRA. Contact People Operations for individualized planning.

---

## 6. Compensation & Salary Reviews

### 6.1 Pay schedule
Salaries are paid **monthly**, on the last business day of each month, via
direct deposit. Payslips are available in the Employee Portal under "Payslips."

### 6.2 Salary review cycle
Formal salary reviews are conducted **bi-annually in January and July**. All
full-time employees who have completed at least 6 months of service are
eligible for review.

### 6.3 Promotion requests
To request a promotion or off-cycle salary review:

1. Schedule a 1:1 with your direct manager at least 30 days before the
   upcoming review cycle.
2. Complete the self-assessment template (available on the intranet).
3. Submit the package to People Operations through the HR Queries portal.

### 6.4 Bonus eligibility
Performance bonuses are calculated annually based on individual and company
performance. Eligible employees receive between **5% and 20% of base salary**
as their target annual bonus, paid in March of the following year.

### 6.5 Employee referral bonus
Employees who refer a successful hire receive:

- **$1,500** for general roles, paid after the new hire completes their
  90-day probation period.
- **$2,000** for engineering, data, and AI/ML roles.
- **$3,000** for senior leadership roles (Director and above).

Referrals must be submitted through the HR portal before the candidate
applies. The referrer must remain employed at Nimbus Labs at the time the
bonus is paid.

## 7. Health & Insurance Benefits

### 7.1 Medical, dental, vision
Comprehensive coverage is available from the first day of employment. The
company pays 90% of the employee premium and 75% of dependent premiums.

### 7.2 401(k) retirement plan
Eligibility begins after 60 days. The company matches **100% of the first 4%**
of base salary contributed. Vesting is immediate.

### 7.3 Life insurance
Basic life insurance equal to 2x annual base salary is provided at no cost.
Supplemental coverage may be purchased.

### 7.4 Short-term & long-term disability
Short-term disability covers up to 60% of base salary for up to 26 weeks. Long-
term disability begins after week 26 and continues to age 65 if needed. Both
are fully employer-paid.

### 7.5 Mental health & wellness
Employees receive an annual **$1,200 wellness stipend** that may be applied to
gym memberships, therapy, mental health apps, or fitness equipment. Receipts
are reimbursed monthly via the HR portal.

---

## 8. Learning & Development

### 8.1 Annual L&D reimbursement
Each full-time employee receives **up to $2,000 per calendar year** for
approved professional development:

- Online courses (Coursera, Udemy, O'Reilly, etc.)
- Industry conferences and workshops
- Certification exams (AWS, GCP, PMP, CFA, etc.)
- Books and learning subscriptions

### 8.2 Approval workflow
Submit course details, cost estimate, and manager approval to People
Operations **before** enrollment. Reimbursement is paid after completion and
proof of payment.

### 8.3 Conference travel
Travel expenses to approved conferences are covered separately under the
travel & expense policy and do not count against the $2,000 L&D budget.

### 8.4 Internal training
All employees are required to complete:

- Code of Conduct training within 30 days of hire (annual refresher)
- Security & Data Privacy training within 30 days of hire (annual refresher)
- Anti-Harassment training within 60 days of hire (every 2 years)

---

## 9. Performance Management

### 9.1 Review cadence
Formal performance reviews are conducted twice per year, aligned with the
salary review cycle (January and July).

### 9.2 Continuous feedback
All employees should have weekly or bi-weekly 1:1s with their manager.
Quarterly check-ins are required at minimum.

### 9.3 Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)
A PIP is a structured 60- or 90-day plan with clear, measurable goals.
Employees on a PIP receive weekly check-ins with their manager and a
designated People Operations partner.

## 10. Code of Conduct & Workplace Policies

### 10.1 Equal opportunity
Nimbus Labs is an equal-opportunity employer. Discrimination or harassment
based on race, color, religion, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation,
national origin, age, disability, genetic information, marital status, or
veteran status is strictly prohibited.

### 10.2 Reporting concerns
Concerns about harassment, discrimination, or other misconduct may be
reported confidentially to:

- Your direct manager
- Any People Operations team member
- The anonymous Ethics Hotline (number listed on the intranet)

All reports are investigated; retaliation against reporters is prohibited and
is itself a terminable offense.

### 10.3 Conflicts of interest
Outside employment, board memberships, or financial interests in companies
that compete with or do business with Nimbus Labs must be disclosed in
writing to People Operations within 14 days of the relationship beginning.

### 10.4 Confidentiality & IP
All employees sign a confidentiality and IP assignment agreement on day one.
Proprietary information may not be shared outside the company.

---

## 11. Travel & Expense Reimbursement

### 11.1 Approved travel
Business travel must be pre-approved by the employee's manager. Book through
the company travel portal where possible.

### 11.2 Daily allowances
Per-diem rates while traveling on company business:

- Domestic: **$75 per day** for meals and incidentals
- International: **$100 per day**

### 11.3 Submission deadline
Expense reports must be submitted within **30 days** of trip completion.
Reimbursements are processed on the next bi-weekly cycle.

### 11.4 Personal mileage
Personal vehicle mileage for business travel is reimbursed at the current IRS
standard rate.

---

## 12. Termination & Exit

### 12.1 Notice period
Employees resigning are asked to provide a minimum of **2 weeks written
notice**. Senior roles (Director and above) are asked to provide 4 weeks.

### 12.2 Final paycheck
Final wages, including unused vacation balance, are paid no later than the
employee's final day or per applicable state law, whichever is sooner.

### 12.3 Exit interview
A confidential exit interview is offered to all departing employees. Feedback
is shared with leadership in aggregate, not by name.

### 12.4 Return of property
All company-issued equipment (laptop, badge, phone) must be returned by the
last day of employment.

Queries** menu in the Employee Portal. People Operations responds within

For confidential or sensitive matters, email **people-ops@nimbuslabs.example**
