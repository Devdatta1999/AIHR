# Nimbus Labs — Mission & Values

## Mission
Nimbus Labs builds AI systems that make hiring faster, fairer, and more human. We believe the best hiring decisions come from a tight loop between human judgment and well-structured data. Our products free recruiters and hiring managers from low-leverage work (resume triage, scheduling, offer paperwork) so they can spend their time on what actually matters: the conversation with the candidate.

## The problem we are solving
Hiring teams at mid-market companies waste ~60% of their cycle on coordination, not on evaluating candidates. Meanwhile candidates drop out of slow pipelines, and bias creeps in at every stage where human attention is thin. Our bet is that AI agents, correctly scoped and fully observable, can take over the mechanical work and give humans richer, less biased signals.

## Company values

### Candor over comfort
We say the real thing in the meeting, not after. Disagreement is a gift. We assume positive intent but demand directness — vague feedback is worse than none.

### Ship small, learn fast
Prefer 4 one-week iterations to one four-week launch. Every feature ships behind a flag. "Done" means running in production with a metric you trust.

### Own the outcome, not the task
Engineers own end-to-end: design → code → deploy → on-call → retro. There is no "throw it over the wall." If your feature hurts a customer, you fix it.

### Customer-obsessed, not customer-led
We listen deeply to customers but we are the experts on what the product should be. We push back when they ask for the wrong thing. We ship surprises that delight.

### Respect the craft
Code review is a love language. We care about tests, observability, naming, and refactoring. Writing ugly code to hit a deadline is a decision you have to justify.

### Default to trust
We hire adults. We don't measure hours, we measure outcomes. Async by default, meetings when needed.

## What great looks like at Nimbus Labs
- You push back on a product decision in a meeting, your counterpart changes their mind, and nothing weird happens afterward.
- You ship a feature end-to-end, including the dashboard that proves it works.
- You pick up an on-call incident that isn't "yours" and drive it to a clean RCA.
- You wrote the RFC that started the conversation; someone else shipped the final version; you celebrated.

## Red flags in candidates
- Blames previous teams or managers without self-reflection.
- Describes work only in terms of tasks ("I was told to…") rather than outcomes.
- Avoids disagreement or softens every opinion into mush.
- Needs a lot of hand-holding to take a first step on ambiguous problems.
- Talks about "their code" as something sacred — no interest in changing it.
